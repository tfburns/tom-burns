Preliminary Report: On the Robustness and Computational Power of Spiking Neural Networks in the Presence of Noise

Abstract:

Abstract:Interest in the power and efficiency of spiking neural networks (SNNs) has been longstanding in the research community, and has attracted growing interest from industry. An oft-cited set of proofs demonstrating the computational power of SNNs is from Maass (1996). However, they rely on a linearity assumption in the post-synaptic response functions of neurons and assume that the system is perfectly noiseless. This is biologically implausible and restricts the proof's generalisability. In this paper, we remove the linearity and noiselessness assumptions and propose solutions which maintain much of the original computational power described in Maass (1996).
